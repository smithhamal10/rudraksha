General Questions

Rudraksha beads are seeds from the Rudraksha tree (Elaeocarpus ganitrus), primarily found in the Himalayan region, Indonesia, and other parts of Southeast Asia. These beads are considered sacred in Hinduism and Buddhism, particularly associated with Lord Shiva. The word "Rudraksha" comes from "Rudra" (another name for Shiva) and "Aksha" (eyes), meaning "tears of Shiva." They are believed to possess spiritual energy that aids in meditation, protection, and overall well-being.

Authentic Rudraksha beads have several identifying features:

  • Natural Lines (Mukhi): Clear, well-defined natural lines running from top to bottom
  • Weight: Natural Rudraksha has a certain weight and density
  • Water Test: Genuine beads sink in water
  • Texture: Natural rough texture with visible pores
  • Certificate: We provide authenticity certificates with detailed specifications

We also offer verification services if you have concerns about your purchase.

Each Mukhi (face) of Rudraksha is associated with different deities and benefits:

  • 1 Mukhi: Lord Shiva - Enhanced concentration, leadership
  • 2 Mukhi: Ardhanareeshwar - Harmony in relationships
  • 3 Mukhi: Agni Dev - Confidence, past karma cleansing
  • 4 Mukhi: Lord Brahma - Knowledge, creativity, communication
  • 5 Mukhi: Kalagni Rudra - Most common, general well-being
  • 6 Mukhi: Lord Kartikeya - Willpower, focus

Higher Mukhi beads (7-21) have increasingly specific spiritual benefits and are rarer.

Wearing Guidelines:

  • Wear touching the skin, preferably around the neck or wrist
  • Face the Rudraksha towards your heart
  • Wear during meditation, prayer, and daily activities
  • Remove during sleep if it feels heavy

Care Instructions:

  • Clean with soft brush and water occasionally
  • Apply a drop of oil (sesame or olive) monthly
  • Avoid exposure to soap, chemicals, and extreme temperatures
  • Store in a clean, sacred space when not wearing

Rudraksha can be worn by anyone regardless of age, gender, caste, or religion. However, traditional guidelines suggest:

  • Maintain physical and mental purity
  • Avoid non-vegetarian food and alcohol while wearing (traditional view)
  • Women may choose to remove during menstruation (optional, based on personal beliefs)
  • Children can wear Rudraksha under parental guidance
  • Most importantly, wear with faith and positive intention

The effectiveness of Rudraksha depends more on your faith and regular wearing than strict rules.
